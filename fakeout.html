Exercise Fake-Out

Learning studio 1: Spring 2020 // Project 1

The big takeaway: in order to succeed, you must first be willing to experience failure. literally, daily.

Reflection

I find reflections to be the most important of any project. Why was it important to me? How did it help me grow as a designer? Why have I included this project on my portfolio? That is why I include my reflection before the project.

Ah, my very first UX project. I include this project on my portfolio not because I am proud of it- but because the memories of it are some of the most important in my life. I didn't know it until years later, but this project solidified my ability to be a UX designer.When I received a failing grade, I could have decided I wasn't cut out for industry..

What happened instead was a need to improve. I needed to get better, I needed to learn what I was doing wrong and fix it. Not everyone is like this, and not everyone feels it so inherently. Like I said, I didn't even realize until years later that this had even happened. This project was my very first encounter with my own growth mindset- and I passed the test I didn't even know I was taking.


Project Overview

The goals we had for this project were: to modify an existing activity tracker application that links to a basic pedometer, empowering users to be healthier and to modify the mental model of what activities are beneficial, rewarding these behaviors over the behavior of cheating.
