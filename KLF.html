KidsLoveFun

experience studio Spring 2021 // January - May 2021

The big takeaway: a well-founded and beautiful story is a critical piece of my design process.

Reflection

I find reflections to be the most important of any project. Why was it important to me? How did it help me grow as a designer? Why have I included this project on my portfolio? That is why I include my reflection before the project.

Creating a story to encourage compassion has become an integral part of how I relate to users. By making the characters come to life through art, I start to connect with them on a level I couldn't achieve before. These characters become so much more than that so quickly- they become simply people, relying on me to understand and empathize with them in order to make their world better.

I learned more about myself and my process than I have in any other project- it is absolutely critical for me to make my users come to life. The more time I spend with them (whether in person or through drawings) the better the product can become.


Project Overview

Out goals for this project was to conduct user research to discover what data regarding the tooth-brushing of their children is most meaningful to parents. Using these insights, we created an app UI to display this data (collected by Tody, the tooth-brushing robot) to parents.

  • See the full documentation >
  • See our final presentation >

In this project, my main role was creating storyboards, and championing presentations (Our final presentation can be found in the link above). I also worked on many individual aspects of the project- such as Figma mockups, stakeholder interviews, and conversing with sponsors about their product.
This project also challenged me in other ways, too: I didn't wholeheartedly agree with our sponsors that their product would be ideal for their users- but my role wasn't to question their product, it was to make an intuitive app.
